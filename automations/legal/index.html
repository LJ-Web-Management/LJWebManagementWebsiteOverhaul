    Legal Automations

    5 done-for-you automations built for the roles that keep this industry running. Book a consultation and we'll tailor any of these to your exact tools.

    Book a Consultation See How It Works
    Attorney

    Matter Profitability & Unbilled Time Alert System

    At the end of each week, this automation pulls every open matter the attorney has billed time to, compares total time captured against the retainer balance or budget estimate, and delivers a per-matter profitability summary. Matters where time captured is tracking to exceed the budget by more than 20% receive an immediate flag so the attorney can have a scope conversation with the client before the overage becomes an invoice dispute. Unbilled time sitting more than 10 days without being applied to an invoice triggers a reminder — because time that isn't captured quickly is time that disappears permanently.

    Tools Required
    Practice management/billing software (e.g., Clio or TimeSolv)Zapier or Make.comGoogle Sheets or Airtable (profitability summary)Slack or email (weekly alert delivery)
    Paralegal

    Discovery Request Tracker & Response Deadline System

    When a discovery request is received and logged, this automation calculates the response deadline based on the applicable rules — federal, state, or local — creates a series of internal preparation deadlines working backward from that date, and assigns tasks to the responsible attorney and paralegal with escalating reminders. Any task that misses its internal deadline triggers an immediate flag to the supervising attorney. Litigation is managed to a calendar, not to whoever remembered to check their inbox that morning.

    Tools Required
    Practice management software (e.g., Clio or MyCase)Zapier or Make.comGoogle Calendar or Outlook (deadline entries)Airtable (discovery tracker)Slack (internal deadline alerts)
    Law Firm Office Manager

    Vendor Invoice Approval & Payment Scheduling System

    Every vendor invoice received by the firm — court reporter, process server, expert witness, office supplies — is automatically routed to the appropriate matter or overhead code, sent to the office manager for one-click approval, and queued for payment on the next scheduled payment run. Invoices that sit unapproved for more than five business days trigger an escalation. At month end, an expense summary by matter and by vendor category is generated automatically, giving the office manager the data needed for budget reviews without exporting anything manually from any system.

    Tools Required
    QuickBooks Online or a legal billing platformZapier or Make.comJotform or email (invoice intake and approval flow)Google Sheets (expense summary)Slack (unapproved invoice escalations)
    Legal Intake Specialist

    Missed Call & After-Hours Lead Recovery System

    Every missed call to the firm's main line triggers an automatic text back to the caller within two minutes, acknowledging the call and asking what they're calling about — converting a missed call into a text conversation the intake specialist can respond to when available. Calls and web form submissions that come in after hours are logged, auto-acknowledged with a clear response time expectation, and added to the intake specialist's morning queue with full context. Firms that adopt this system stop losing prospective clients to competitors who answered first, because the firm's first response now goes out in seconds regardless of the hour.

    Tools Required
    Phone system with missed call webhook (e.g., RingCentral or Grasshopper)Zapier or Make.comTwilio (automatic text-back)CRM (e.g., Lawmatics or Clio Grow)Slack (morning queue summary)
    Legal Marketing Manager

    Referral Partner Tracking & Nurture System

    Every time a new client lists a referral source during intake, the system logs the referring attorney, accountant, or business contact, updates their referral count in the CRM, and automatically triggers a personalized thank-you email from the firm. Referral partners who haven't sent a case in 90 days receive a gentle check-in from the marketing manager — turning the referral network from a mental note into a systematically managed revenue channel.

    Tools Required
    CRM (e.g., Clio Grow or HubSpot)Zapier or Make.comMailchimp or ActiveCampaignJotform (intake source tracking)Google Sheets (referral dashboard)
